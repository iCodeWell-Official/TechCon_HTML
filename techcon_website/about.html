History

The Origins and Evolution of TechCon

TechCon, one of the most significant annual technology conventions, has grown from a small gathering of industry enthusiasts to a global event that shapes the future of technology. Its journey mirrors the rapid advancements in the tech world, making it an essential platform for innovators, developers, and businesses alike.

Origins

TechCon's humble beginnings trace back to the early 1990s when a small group of tech developers and engineers gathered to share ideas about the growing potential of personal computers and the Internet. Originally focused on local innovations, the convention was more like a meetup for developers interested in programming languages, operating systems, and early web technologies. It was known as “The Tech Meetup” and held in Silicon Valley, an emerging tech hub at the time.

Looking Ahead

Today, TechCon is more than just an event—it is a cornerstone of the tech industry. It continues to evolve, focusing on sustainability, inclusivity, and the social impact of technology. With each passing year, the convention fosters collaboration, sparks innovation, and shapes the future of technology on a global scale. As the tech landscape continues to change, so too will TechCon, remaining at the forefront of technological evolution.

Mission

TechCon aims to foster innovation, collaboration, and inclusivity in the tech industry. The conference serves as a platform to:

  • Inspire: Showcase cutting-edge technologies and ideas that drive the future.
  • Connect: Bring together developers, entrepreneurs, and thought leaders to network and collaborate.
  • Educate: Offer workshops, talks, and hands-on sessions to expand knowledge and skills.
  • Empower: Provide resources and opportunities for individuals and companies to grow and innovate.

Past Speakers

Greg Yeric

Greg Yeric is the Director of Research at the CHIPS National Semiconductor Technology Center Program. He has a strong background in semiconductor technology, starting his career at Motorola in embedded non-volatile memory process integration. He later co-founded Cerfe Labs and worked extensively at Arm Holdings, where he helped pioneer Design-Technology Co-Optimization (DTCO). Greg has contributed to the advancement of microelectronics and semiconductor processes, holding over 20 patents and giving invited talks at major industry conferences.

Simon Segars

Simon Segars was the CEO of Arm Holdings, where he played a pivotal role in advancing chip architecture and processor design that has become integral to smartphones, tablets, and other devices globally. Under his leadership, Arm made significant strides in the IoT and automotive sectors. He is recognized for driving Arm’s business expansion and technological innovations.

Jonathan Koomey

Jonathan Koomey is an influential researcher in energy efficiency and environmental impacts of technology, renowned for his analysis of energy consumption in computing. He formulated “Koomey’s Law,” which observes that the energy efficiency of computing doubles roughly every 1.5 years, similar to Moore’s Law. His work, including key reports on data center energy use and cloud computing, has significantly shaped industry practices and policies, advocating for more sustainable and efficient technology solutions.
